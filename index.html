So who I am ?

A 20 years-old, Indian-Italian, with the vision of building a business empire. 

I was born on 28/06/2002 in the famous Italian district called Vicenza, the city of gold known for its trade in precious metals and worldwide famous leather industry. Moreover, I am a powerful mix between Haryanvi and Punjabi. What else do people need ?!

I love self-learning things and the biggest reason is that I am curious person, sit with me for an hour or two and i'll make you question our very own existence (a big thanks to the Socratic method which I learned at the age of 7). 

When my family started its first business (in partnership) in India (today with a turnover of 100+ crore INR) when I was around 10 I got deeply interested in the world of business.  And here I am today studying towards my BA in Management and Economics.

​

So now that you know a bit about me let's take a deeper look into my life. 

 

Where have I worked ?

Now its time to explore my work experience gained in different countries, in different roles, and in different times...

My permanent job

There are two big issues in Italy: language and complex bureocracy. I helped (and still help) local people in diffrent matters from helping them out to getting their documetns right, to helping local indian businesses setting their social media presence (just starting not managing). Moreover is there any issue with computer ? Manpreet is here. You want to install CCTV cameras ? Manpreet is here. All free of cost.

My first job

I lived in the UK for around a year in London. While I was studying at the college I got a side job at a local indian food store. It was a fun job since I got the chance to interact with different people, managing inventory, taking deliveries and so on. 

Experience in our own companies

Just before COVID-19 pandemic and the start of my first year of uni I worked along my father in the different departments of our companies (mainlyoperations, finance, quality). We are in the plastic and EPS (Expanded Polystyrene) manufacturing business working for companies such as Samsung and Panasonic. It allowed me to see from the inside (from the bottom to the top) how businesses operate in a higly complex environment. And to further strengthen this experience I decided to pursue a degree in management and economics. 

My education and certifications
(not all just the main ones)

BA in Management and Economics from The Open University Business School

A world class business degree from a triple accredited business school that puts it in the top 1%. Not based on theory but on practice with an extensive use of case study from all kinds of businesses (from start-ups to MNCs) in all different kinds of contexts.

Certification in Lean Manufacturing from Technical University of Munich 

A very interesting course teaching the foundations of lean manufacturing such as cpacity analysis, setup time reductions, kaizen etc.

Certification in project management from the University of Adelaide

A course that teaches the very basics of how to manage projects, for example with the use of Gantt charts.
